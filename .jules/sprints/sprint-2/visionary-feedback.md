## Suggestion for Amplification

As you define the label structure, consider adding a specific category to capture the *semantic* nature of an issue. For example:

- `type: decision`: Marks an issue where a key architectural or product decision was made.
- `type: learning`: Marks an issue that resulted in a significant learning or a refactoring based on a failed experiment.
- `type: feature-request`: Captures user needs and ideas.

By adding this semantic layer, your triage process won't just be cleaning the backlog; you'll be actively curating the raw material for a system that can automatically answer questions like:
- "What were the last 3 major architectural decisions we made?"
- "Show me all the learnings related to the database."

This small addition transforms a cleanup task into a strategic knowledge-capture process, directly accelerating the moonshot vision of a self-aware project memory.
